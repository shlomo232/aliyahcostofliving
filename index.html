Cost of living & Aliyah

Are you thinking of making aliyah, but worried about how your standard of living will translate to a different country?

Use this tool to estimate your cost of living in the US and Israel.

The calculations are for 2 adults and any number of kids.

1. Some background:

Many of these calculations are based on statistics for the "average" person or family. Of course, nobody is exactly average. Costs vary for many reasons, for example:

  • Location (Manhattan vs Mississippi, Tel Aviv vs Beer Sheva)
  • Social class and communal standards
  • Individual needs and tastes
  • Ability to budget
So don't rely on these numbers as an exact prediction of your expenses in a given place. Do rely on them as an approximate comparison of the costs in two different places.

4. Follow-up:

This should help you compare your expenses in the US and Israel. The other side of the coin is your income.

If you're thinking of aliyah, ask some olim in your future profession what you can expect to work in and earn. This varies a lot by person and career, but someone who's already "in the field" can usually give you a good idea of what to expect.

Once you have a good guess at your future salary and expenses, you can be confident about whether aliyah will be financially successful for you.
